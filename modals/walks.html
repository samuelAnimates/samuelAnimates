WALKS THROUGH CAMBRIDGE

( ^^ In progress... )

2011, USA

Site-specific public art installation.

Materials: yard signs and paper.

Overview


A public art installation in Harvard Yard, focusing on the experience transitioning to life on Harvard's campus and entering the "Harvard Bubble".

Context


Supported by an ARTS FIRST '11 grant from Harvard's Office for the Arts.
Made possible by the participation of: Jake, Cherish, Robert, Khin, and Dipona.
Thank you.

Questions I Set Out to Answer:

  • How can I use physical space to guide people through a specific thought process, when I have them as a captive audience + when I don't?
  • How can I represent the major themes of a group's lived experiences, + make them accessible to others?

Process

I took a few groups of participants on a 30-minute silent walk in one direction, away from the freshman dorms of Harvard Yard. Afterwards, I interviewed them about their experience on the walk, as well as their experience adjusting to life on campus. I then compiled key quotes and major themes to write their experiences into two paragraphs. I divided the text into sentences on yard signs so viewers would read one line at a time as they walked along a path within Harvard Yard.

It was sunny and warm as we walked straight past the Northwest Labs, to places we’ve never seen—it was a little amazing. Walking eventually became automatic, like riding a train looking at the scenery. Past the cobblestone and buildings with character is a different world in an actual neighborhood. The whole time I felt like I was in one of those movies where the main character goes to an alternate universe: they can see/hear everything going on, but they can’t interact with it.


I liked Cambridge immediately: the college town experience, with Boston right there as an option as well. But it doesn’t feel much different from Ann Arbor, aside from a big city right there. It feels less real somehow. I guess because the Square is filled with shops and boutiques, not like a big grocery store or something. Maybe it’s just because I haven’t explored Cambridge or maybe I don’t use it as a real town. In some ways I feel like living somewhere is supposed to mean grocery shopping. But I mean I don’t know what that means if I’m not living here.


Motivation

To be honest, this project was motivated by a sense of isolation during my first year at Harvard. As many other first-gneration college students can attest to, the campus of an "elite" institution can be a very isolating place. I felt myself becoming disconnected from things going on at home, without a sense of belonging in my new place of residence to try to make up for it. I started going on walks to help remind myself of my own power in the situation. Each time I chose to go on a long walk was a chance to remind myself of the entire world going on outside of Harvard's sphere of influence. Each time I walked back was a chance to remind myself that I was making a continuous choice to stay and pursue my passions there, a power over my own path.


I took on this project to share this process with others; through opening up with other people in this way, I learned I wasn't alone in feeling this way. I hope that by putting this project on display in Harvard Yard, I was able to help others come to the same conclusion.
