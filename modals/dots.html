CONNECT THE DOTS!

(^^ In progress...)

2017, USA

Site-specific art installation in two public bathrooms in a Harvard College dorm.

Materials: paper, pens, and ribbon.

Overview


Site-specific, interactive installation on the topics of handwashing + shared space.

Context


A big thanks to the anonymous participants and to Quincy House for accommodating me.

This work was made possible by a grant from the General Education program at Harvard University.

Process

I designed and printed a connect-the-dots banner to hang in two public bathrooms. After hanging them up on the walls and providing pens, I let the particpants shape what installation turned into.


Motivation

This project came from seeing firsthand a lack of handwashing in this very busy bathroom in a centrally-located Harvard dorm. Given the fact that were already signs reminding people to wash their hands, I created a way to remind people they had entered a shared space. Their current environment was shaped by by many others' contributionsto to it before them, and the viewer's current actions will shape the environment for the next person who enters.
