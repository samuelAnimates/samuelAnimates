CONNECT THE DOTS!

2013, USA

Site-specific art installation in two public bathrooms in a Harvard College dorm.

Materials: paper, pens, tape, and ribbon.

Personal Take


Building a platform, high-tech or low-, creates a space to make hidden process visible, like gradual collective action in a shared space.

Acknowledgment

  • Made possible by anonymous participants and by Quincy House for accommodating me.
  • This work was made possible by a grant from the General Education program at Harvard University.

Implications for Future Work

  • Giving up personal control in an intervention can allow the designer to learn new lessons, even in a familiar environment.
  • Creating a self-contained platform for interactions can lead to outputs that are understandable from an outside perspective, and ready for dissemination.

Process

I designed and printed a connect-the-dots banner to hang in two public bathrooms. After hanging them up on the walls and providing pens, I let anonymous classmates shape the installation over the course of 2 weeks.


Motivation

This project came from seeing firsthand a lack of handwashing in this very busy bathroom in a centrally-located Harvard dorm. Given the fact that were already signs reminding people to wash their hands, I created a way to remind people that they were in a shared space. Their current environment was shaped by by many others' interactions with it before them, and their own actions will shape the environment for the next person who enters.
