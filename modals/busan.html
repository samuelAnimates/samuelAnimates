BY FOOT:
BUSAN AND THE SEA

2015, SOUTH KOREA/USA

Animation (stop-motion, pixilation), live action video.

04min 22s. (Trailer above)

Synopsis

An animated travel doc, depicting a first-person view of solo travel through Busan, South Korea.

Recognition

Official Latino Short Film Festival, Exceptional Artistry award nomination, New York, NY, 2017.

SHORT to the Point, official selection, 2017.

Process

I made this film while living out of a hostel, fresh from leaving a job, with no idea of what I was going to do next.


Every day I got up, packed up my equipment into a bright orange backpack, and took the bus/subway to somewhere new in the city. Sometimes I went into the day with a plan for my photograpghy, based off of online research or passing by the location earlier. More often, I decided to take each visit one step at a time.


Despite its energetic pace, this film required a lot of slow, purposeful movement. Sometimes that meant taking a few steps, snapping a photo, and repeating until I got tired. Other times, that meant using a sound recorder to listen carefully to the environment around me, chasing down a soundscape that felt unique to that specific part of the city. Other times, it meant doubling back over my path to take photos of interesting recurring patterns in the environment I noticed through my slow walking.


In the end, this film turned into an ode to solo travel, and a reminder from my past self to pay more attention to the spaces I'm in. It also put the fire in my belly to keep exploring animation as a documentary method, and discover ways to put the power of reflective art and documentary into more people's hands
